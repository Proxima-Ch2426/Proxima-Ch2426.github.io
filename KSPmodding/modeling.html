Notes

This tutorial will NOT teach you how to use Blender, there are plenty of toridal tutorials on YouTube to learn from.

Community standards

Stack parts INCLUSIVE of 3.75 m and below should be 24 sided cylinders, with anything larger being 48 sided cylinders.

Cylinder edges should be along the cardinal axes (not the flat sides, see image below).

KSP's stock part scale is 0.625 times that of real world scale, multiply the real scale of your part by 0.625 and choose the closest diameter below. (You can use your own diameter if you want to)

Standrd part diameters:
  • 0.3125 m
  • 0.625 m
  • 0.9375 m
  • 1.25 m
  • 1.5 m
  • 1.875 m
  • 2.5 m
  • 3.125 m
  • 3.75 m
  • 4.375 m
  • 5 m
  • 6.25 m
  • 7.5 m
  • 10 m

Modeling tips

It's better to parent objects rather than merge them as it is easier for you and other modders to modifiy your models.

KSP does not support concave colliders, use multiple convex colliders. Also try to use Unity's built in colldiers when you can, for example landing legs.

Keep in mind of your polycount, try not to have unneccessary geometry. A good rule of thumb is to not have a single part exceed 20k triangles.
